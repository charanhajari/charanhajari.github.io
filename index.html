Deepfake Detection System: A National Security Solution

This project addresses the critical challenge of identifying maliciously generated deepfake images and videos, a growing threat to national security and public trust.

The Problem:

The proliferation of sophisticated deepfake technology has made it increasingly difficult to discern authentic media from fabricated content. This poses significant risks in areas like misinformation campaigns, fraud, and identity impersonation, demanding robust detection mechanisms.

Your Role & Contribution:

As the lead developer, I designed and implemented the core detection algorithms. My responsibilities included data preprocessing, model selection, training, and optimizing the system for efficiency and accuracy. I focused on integrating various detection techniques to enhance reliability.

Technologies Used:

Python, TensorFlow/Keras, OpenCV, NumPy, specific image processing libraries (e.g., PIL).

Your Process & Approach:

My approach involved a multi-faceted detection strategy. Initially, I focused on feature extraction from facial manipulations and inconsistencies in video frames. I explored various machine learning models, including Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), trained on diverse datasets of real and deepfake media. Post-processing techniques were applied to refine detection results and reduce false positives. Iterative testing and model refinement were crucial steps to improve the system's robustness against different deepfake generation methods.

Solution & Impact:

The developed system effectively analyzes visual cues and temporal inconsistencies within media to flag potential deepfakes. It achieved a high detection accuracy rate during testing, demonstrating its potential as a valuable tool for intelligence agencies and media verification platforms. This project showcases my ability to apply advanced AI/ML concepts to solve critical cybersecurity challenges.

Hacking Tool: Keylogger & Screenshot - Ethical Hacking Simulation

This project involved engineering a stealthy tool for ethical hacking simulations, designed to capture keylogs and screenshots for penetration testing exercises.

The Problem:

Understanding the techniques used by malicious actors is crucial for effective defense. This project aimed to demonstrate the ease with which sensitive information can be compromised through seemingly simple tools, aiding in the development of countermeasures.

Your Role & Contribution:

I was responsible for the full development lifecycle of this tool, from conceptualization and design to implementation and testing. This included researching stealth techniques and data exfiltration methods.

Technologies Used:

Python, `pynput` (for keylogging), `Pillow` (for screenshots), socket programming (for data transfer).

Your Process & Approach:

The development involved creating a listener for keyboard events and integrating a module to capture desktop screenshots at defined intervals. The captured data was then securely transmitted to a remote server. Focus was placed on making the tool lightweight and evasive, mimicking real-world attack vectors in a controlled environment. Ethical considerations and responsible use were paramount throughout the development process.

Solution & Impact:

The functional keylogger and screenshot tool successfully demonstrated common exfiltration techniques. This project provided invaluable insights into user monitoring vulnerabilities and underscored the importance of robust endpoint security, password hygiene, and vigilance against suspicious software. It serves as a powerful learning tool for understanding adversary tactics.
